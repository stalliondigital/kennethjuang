Reflection

With COVID-19, we were limited to only a day’s worth of footage to work with, thus, we had to adapt our narrative and the b-roll footage with it. In doing so, we had to shift our original plan of highlighting the post production process and focus more on the product and brand. Because of this, we had to do a lot of narrative editing which meant a lot of time was put into audio cutting and smoothening Melody’s words to sound natural.

This course usually comes with a showcase at the end of the semester with awards given out at the end of it. Although we didn’t get any verification, I personally think this was a successful project because we effectively communicated Melody’s values as a businesswoman and her passion for sustainability with an art direction that reflected the romantic style of her brand and products. Moving forward, I would like to continue working on developing my skills with narrative communication and working with brands on projects like this.

Context

A micro-documentary owner and solopreneur of MalaTheBrand, Melody Lim, documenting the whys of her business along with her creative process and the inspirations behind her soy candles.
